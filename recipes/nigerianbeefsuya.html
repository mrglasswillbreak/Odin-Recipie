Oven Nigerian Beef Suya


Beef suya is thinly sliced meat roasted over high heat until lightly charred, its surface coated in yaji—a peanut-based spice blend that delivers heat, smokiness, and nutty depth. The beef remains tender while the exterior develops a dry, aromatic crust. Traditionally served with sliced onions and tomatoes, suya is bold, direct, and intensely savory, designed to be eaten hot and immediately.

Ingridients

  • 1 kg beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons ground roasted peanuts
  • 1 tablespoon of papeika
  • 1 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F).
  2. Mix peanuts, spices, salt, and oil into a dry paste.
  3. Coat beef slices evenly.
  4. Spread beef on a wire rack over a baking tray.
  5. Roast 12–15 minutes, flipping once, until slightly charred at edges.

Whay This Works

The peanut-based spice mix toasts during cooking, producing deep, smoky flavor without needing a marinade.

Serbing Notes

Serve with sliced onions, tomatoes, and extra suya spice on the side.
